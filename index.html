National Parks and Monuments of the Rocky Mountain West

University of Kentucky Geography

The Yellowstone National Park Protection Act was signed into law by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, thus establishing the world's first national park. When the park was established the vision was that Yellowstone National Park would exist without cost to the government. However, without funding for basic infrastructure or law enforcement officers, protecting wildlife from poachers and the natural features from vandalism proved difficult. President Woodrow Wilson established the National Parks Service on August 25, 1916 with the mission of preserving unimpaired the natural and cultural resources and values of the National Park System for the enjoyment, education, and inspiration of this and future generations. Since inception, national park patronage has continued to grow. In 2018, more than 85 million people visit national parks alone. The establishment of Yellowstone National Park, and later, the National Park Service have successfully preserved some of America's most iconic scenery. The awe-inspiring beauty of their vistas leaves an indelible mark. For more information about our national parks please visit the National Park Service website.

National Parks and National Monuments extracted from the Domestic and Antarctic Names (State and Topical Gazetteer) published by the United States Board on Geographic Names. (geonames.usgs.gov) Data accessed December, 2019. Contours were extracted from a USGS NED 1/3 arc-second raster digital elevation model. This data can be accessed at the USGS 3D Elevation Program (usgs.gov)

Tools used in this project

  • PostGIS was used to store/query the national dataset.
  • Data is shown on Mapbox web map with geolocation services enabled. If the map got popular it would require a fee to use.
  • GDAL and QGIS 3.10 to convert raster elevation units and create contours.
